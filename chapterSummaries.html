Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter 1
  • The novel opens with the description of Lieutenant Jimmy Cross' unreturned love of a girl named Martha.
  • They write back and forth and Cross keeps her letters in his nap sack and hopes that she will feel the same way one day.
  • Cross states that they only went on one date and that he made a move that she did not approve of and now he wishes he had kept doing it.
  • O'Brien, the narrator, lists the things that they men of his company carry with them in Vietnam.
  • Most are just basic essentials like marijuana, gum, or knives but others carry more meaningful items with sentimental value.
  • For example, Henry Dobbins carries the pantyhose of his girlfriend on a count of being superstitious. Ted Lavender, marijuana and tranquilizers in order to stay calm and collective, And Kiowa a copy of the New Testament.
  • All the men carry a multi purpose poncho and a M-16 Rifle if they are low ranking soldiers.
  • Lieutenant Cross carries maps and such while Rat Kiley, company medic, carries painkillers and bandages.
  • While Cross is day dreaming about "his Martha" Lavender gets shot then dies.
  • After Lavender's body is taken away the Alpha Company destroys the village of Than Khe by burning it and killing animals.
  • At the end of the day at the Company's campsite, Cross cries and Kiowa and Norman Bowker discuss life.
  • The next morning Cross burns his memories of Martha which includes her letters and two pictures, he then takes blame for the death of their friend and claims he will never day dream again.

  • Chapter 4
  • O'Brien tells a story that he claims none of his family knows that occurred during the summer of 1968
  • On June 17th, 1968, O'Brien receives his draft notice for Vietnam. This happens one month after he had graduated from Macalester College, Phi Beta Kappa, summa cum laude, and president of his student body.
  • Since O'Brien disagrees with the war he is puzzled on what to do.
  • After he received his draft notice his community puts enormous pressure on him to go fight in Vietnam when he wants to flee.
  • His summer is spent working for a meat packing plant in his town of Worthington, Minnesota where he uses a water gun to remove blood clots from pigs.
  • He weighs every option possible such as finishing school, claiming ill, or principle alone.
  • His thought of fleeing to Canada grows stronger but he doesn't want to disappoint the people of Worthington.
  • O'Brien finally gives in and leaves for Canada after writing a note to his parents.
  • He finds himself along the Rainy River, which splits American and Canada and ends up staying for six days at the Tip Top Lodge where he meets the owner Elroy Berdahl.
  • They bond over that time and O'Brien states that even though he never told Elroy his reason for heading to Canada, he feels he already knew
  • When O'Brien brings up the topic of his bill for staying there he ends up getting two hundred dollars from Elroy.
  • He asks the reader to day dream with him and imagine sitting on the dock of the Tip Top Lodge and telling his parents he is sorry through a letter.
  • The final day at the lodge he goes fishing with Elroy. When they stop O'Brien sees the Canadian shoreline and cries until he decides to go home and then to war.
  • The next day he is gone and the two hundred dollars that was given to him by Elroy is sitting on the sink.
  • He heads to Vietna
