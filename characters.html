Characters

  • Tim O'Brien
  • Narrator and protagonist of stories.
  • A pacifist that uses his obligation to his family and country to explain his part in the war.
  • Uses story telling ability to handle guilt and confusion from the horrendous things he saw in Vietnam such as the death of friends and even his own killing of a Viet Cong soldier.
  • Jimmy Cross
  • A well-intentioned Lieutenant of the Alpha Company.
  • Responsible for leading the men of his group but have doubts.
  • Feels guilty since his fellow Alpha Company soldiers Ted Lavender and Kiowa were killed because of his love problems with Martha and his habit of following orders when his gut tells him otherwise.
  • Mitchell Sanders
  • One of the most popular soldiers in the war.
  • A nice hardworking person that is very fair minded.
  • Looked upon as a father figure because of his father like characteristics.
  • Makes a keen impression on O'Brien with his discussions on the nuances of his stories.
  • His idea of stories do not always match up with O'Brien's
  • Kiowa
  • Best friends with O'Brien
  • Has morals and is very rational and low key even though they are in a war zone.
  • His death is a part of three different stories where the mistake of camping in a sewage field gets him killed.
  • His death provides an example of the displeasure of war.
  • Kiowa's death shows to be more important that his life span in the book.
  • Norman Bowker
  • A quiet and humble character.
  • Manifests the damages that come with war even when the war is over.
  • The death of Kiowa affects him greatly.
  • In the chapter "Notes" he illustrates the significance of story telling for the process of healing.
  • Henry Dobbins
  • The big but kind machine gunner for the platoon.
  • His size paradoxes personality as he is an easygoing, decent person.
  • Bob "Rat" Kiley
  • The platoon's medic
  • Well known for blowing off his own toe in order to leave the war early because of the stress of his job in the war.
  • Served earlier in the setting of "Sweetheart of the Song Tra Bong", Chu Lai.
  • Respected by the men and O'Brien for his skills as a medic
  • Bobby Jorgenson mistreats him when he is shot for the second time and requires aid.
  • Curt Lemon
  • Immature member of the Alpha Company
  • Killed while playing catch with a hand grenade
  • O'Brien does not show great appeal for Lemon but does regret his death due to its avoid ability.
  • His immaturity is a prime example of the young soldiers in the war.
  • Ted Lavender
  • Another young and immature soldier with a tragic and preventable death.
  • His death epitomizes the disposal of humans during needless war.
  • Is so scared during the company's presence outside Than Khe that he takes tranquilizers to get a hold of his emotions.
  • Lee Strunk
  • Well known for his alteration with his soldier Dave Jensen that results in his broken nose.
  • Has a pact with Dave Jensen that if one is to get severely hurt in battle the other is to put him out of his misery.
  • Dave Jensen
  • Is happy that he does not have to mourn over the death of his friend because he broke their pact and did not kill his friend.
  • Azar
  • Treats his fellow soldiers and Vietnamese civilians with no respect and even taunts them.
  • Uses his mean and savage personality as a defense mechanism that we see unravel towards the end of the novel when he assists his company in retrieving Kiowa's dead body
  • Bobby Jorgenson
  • Replaces Rat Kiley as the Company's medic.
  • Treats O'Brien unprofessionally when he is shot for the second time, which results in some revenge.
  • Elroy Berdahl
  • Owns the Tip Top Lodge that resides on the Rainy River close to the Canadian border.
  • Is seen as almost a father figure towards O'Brien while he decides if he will fight in the war or run to Canada.
  • Kathleen
  • The only daughter that O'Brien talks about during the novel.
  • Even though O'Brien feels she should not know all of the details of his experience in the war, it teaches him to see a new side of his experience.
  • Mary Anne Bell
  • The girl friend of Mark Fossie.
  • When she gets to Vietnam she is very innocent but quickly loses herself in the darkness.
  • Mark Fossie
  • A medic from Rat Kiley's prior Company.
  • Is crushed when he discovers that his girlfriend is more interested in joining the Green Berets that planning their post war lives.
  • Linda
  • A 5th grade girl who dies of a brain tumor that O'Brien calls his first love.
  • Her death teaches O'Brien about tragedy and how telling stories helps the healing process by keeping good memories alive.
